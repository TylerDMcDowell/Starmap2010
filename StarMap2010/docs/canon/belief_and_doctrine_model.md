# Belief and Doctrine in the Astraeum
Status: CANON  
Scope: Player-facing, cultural, institutional  

This document defines how belief systems function within the Astraeum without invoking metaphysical causation.

---

## Core Principle

The universe does not respond to belief.

Civilizations do.

---

## Functional Belief

Belief systems emerge wherever:
- systems are dangerous
- outcomes are probabilistic
- failure is catastrophic
- restraint is unpopular

Belief survives when it enforces behavior that reduces casualty rates.

---

## Ritual as Behavioral Control

Across species and cultures, belief systems encode:
- waiting
- verification
- redundancy
- humility
- refusal to proceed under pressure

These behaviors may be framed as:
- reverence
- harmony
- obedience
- professionalism
- superstition

The framing varies.
The outcome converges.

## Failure Modes

Belief systems fail in predictable ways:

### Fanaticism
- belief is treated as evaluative
- outcomes are moralized
- risk becomes proof

### Reform Collapse
- ritual is stripped without replacement
- enforcement weakens
- casualty rates spike

### Political Capture
- belief becomes a tool of sovereignty
- doctrine is bent to advantage
- infrastructure absorbs the cost

---

## Multiplicity of Belief

The Lumenary model is dominant but not exclusive.

Other belief systems exist:
- professionalized procedural cultures
- fatalistic doctrines
- rationalist reform movements
- localized spacer traditions

Contradiction is normal.
Uniformity is suspicious.

---

## Design Constraint

At no point should belief:
- guarantee success
- alter physics
- produce supernatural effects

Belief succeeds by shaping behavior, not reality.

This document constrains interpretation.
